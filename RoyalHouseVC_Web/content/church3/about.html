ABOUT ROYALHOUSE CHAPEL INTERNATIONAL VICTORY CENTER

A FEW WORDS ABOUT US

Royalhouse Chapel International in Connecticut was the very first mission to be planted in the USA. It was from the Connecticut Mission that all the other missions were birthed. Indeed, the church in Connecticut was the very first international mission to be planted across the world.

The Beginning Stages
October 1998 - May 2000; Manchester, CT

In October 1998, the Apostle-General first visited Connecticut at the invitation of some friends as the main speaker at a revival in East Hartford. In July 1999, some early interest was established in terms of wanting to plant a church in Connecticut, and so, after much prayer, Royalhouse Chapel was started in the basement of the Church of the Nazarene, 218 Main Street, Manchester, CT. The church met and fellowshipped at this location until May 2000, when some of the members decided to break off and start their own ministry. The remaining members of Royalhouse Chapel, 7 people in total, led by the then resident Pastor, Rev. Charles Benneh, began to meet at 65 Enfield Avenue, Hartford, CT, the home of one of the church leaders.

May 2000 - Fall 2004 (Manchester, CT)

From about May 2000 to late 2004, the church met at 856 Main Street, Manchester, CT. Those early years were very challenging, as we attempted to establish the vision of Royalhouse Chapel in an area that had not previously heard of us.

During these years, the church was pastored by Rev. Charles Benneh, and subsequently by Rev. Joseph Essilfie, who transitioned to New York in 2004 to start the church plant there. This transition to New York essentially left the church without a pastor, which resulted in Rev. Thomas stepping up to steer the ministry and keep it afloat, aided by a few committed members.

Fall 2004- October 2006 (Super 8 Motel, Manchester, CT)

In late 2004, the church was forced to relocate to a temporary meeting location at the Super 8 Motel, also in Manchester, CT 06040. This move was necessitated due to leasing terms dictated by our previous landlord that were restrictive and not in the best interest of the church. The Super 8 Motel became our meeting place twice a week, on Fridays and Sundays, for two years.

During this time, the then General Secretary of Royalhouse Chapel, Rev. Dalkeith Amanor, as well as Rev. Charles Benneh, served temporary pastoral assignments over the ministry.

These were tumultuous years of instability for the church, and the resolve and the commitment to the vision of Royalhouse was heavily tested. For the majority of these years, a group of young professionals, led by Rev. Thomas Brew, were the ones who kept the ministry alive and prevented it from closing down.

October 2006- March 2007 (East Hartford, CT)

In August 2006, the church identified a property for rent at 1221 Burnside Avenue, East Hartford, CT. Although the building was not too spacious, the location was very accessible for most of the members. After raising the funds to renovate the space, we had our first service there on October 8th, 2006, to the glory of God. Our joy was however shortlived, as we were notified by the Town of East Hartford that we could no longer continue to meet there due to a rule in the code book that had been overlooked. Due to this technicality, we were forced to move out of that location after just a few months.

April 2007- June 2010 (East Windsor, CT)

On April 1, 2007, we signed the lease to a new meeting place located at 53 Bridge Street, East Windsor, CT, where we stayed until June 30, 2010. In October 2008, the church adopted a multi-location expansion strategy, and opened a branch in Hartford, CT, pastored by Rev. Peter Parkins, who had also served time pastoring the church from late 2006 to 2008.

At the same time, in October 2008, Rev. Thomas, who had been an active part of the ministry since 2002, was officially handed the reins of the church in East Windsor, and installed as the Snr. Pastor. In November 2009, he was formally inducted as the pastor of the church. This ended the era of many different pastors being brought in over the years for temporary assignments in leading the church and ushered the church into a season of relative calm and stability.

July 2010- May 2023 (South Windsor, CT)

Although the East Windsor location served us well, it was geographically too far for most of our members to drive to. In addition to that, the layout of the building was not suitable in terms of attracting a wide demographic of people. Consequently, a decision was made by leadership to look for a location that was more central to where most of our congregation was coming from, and which was also bigger, in order to accommodate where we believed God was taking us.

We experienced God's faithfulness in a unique way at this time. Led again by Rev. Thomas, the church members congregated at the new site one Sunday in May 2010 to anoint and to pray over the grounds. It was at this meeting that Rev. Thomas laid out the vision to the congregation for what he wanted to accomplish. Although there were some naysayers, a vast majority of our members bought into this vision, contributing generously and raising the money needed to renovate the space at 52A Connecticut Avenue and make it our new home.

Our first church service at this location was held in July 2010, with about 35 people in attendance, and Rev. Dalkeith Amanor as the Guest Speaker for that opening weekend. In November 2009, the church space was dedicated to the Lord by the Apostle-General, and the name 'Victory Center' bestowed on the church by the leading of the Holy Spirit. We also inducted church officers in November 2012 to help stabilize and bring structure to the ministry.

Under Rev. Thomas' leadership, and ably supported by First Lady Ps. Mama Ama Brew, the church stabilized and grew during this time, becoming a thriving multi-cultural congregation, touching and transforming lives, and empowering people from all walks of life to live victoriously for Jesus Christ. It was at this place that God showed His power mightily, and many lives were saved and transformed to the glory of God. Although the church went through many diverse challenges and storms, His faithfulness remained steadfast and unchangeable. We purchased two 15 passenger vans to be used for campus ministry initiatives, and expanded our reach and influence in the community.

May 2023 to Present (East Hartford, CT)

It had always been Rev. Thomas dream and desire for the church in Connecticut to have a place to call home. Having lived through the many changing scenes of tumult and chaos the church had suffered, he firmly believed that God would use him to lead the church to its 'promised land'. After searching for many years, the church finally located a property at 1120 Silver Lane, East Hartford, CT 06118 that would be suitable for its need. Rev. Thomas led the church in prayer concerning the property and aggressively mobilized resources to position us to make the purchase. Through a massive fundraising effort, and through God's providence, the sellers accepted our offer out of twelve others, and we were able to close on the property on February 3, 2023. Valued at $1.5 million, and with over $600, 000 worth of renovations poured into it, this edifice now stands as the new home of Royalhouse Chapel, Victory Center, and will now serve as a place of transformation, community impact, a home for all, and the dwelling place of the most high God.

We continue to trust God in our relentless pursuit of souls for Christ in the community and surrounding areas, bringing them into the sheepfold, with the sole purpose of restoring them, transforming them, unearthing their God-give potential, enabling them to discover their God-given purpose, and equipping them to live victoriously for Christ. This is the essence of our church's mission, which is to touch our generation with the power of God.
